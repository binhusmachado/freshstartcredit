Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about credit repair and our services.

General Questions

Is credit repair legal?

Yes, credit repair is completely legal. The Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA) gives consumers the right to dispute inaccurate information on their credit reports. Credit repair companies simply help you exercise these legal rights.

At Fresh Start Credit Solutions, we strictly comply with the Credit Repair Organizations Act (CROA) and all state regulations. We provide all required disclosures and never make false promises.

How long does credit repair take?

The timeline varies depending on your specific situation, but here's what you can generally expect:

  • First results: 30-45 days after disputes are submitted
  • Significant improvement: 3-6 months for most clients
  • Full results: 6-12 months for complex cases

Credit bureaus have 30 days to investigate disputes (45 days in certain cases). Multiple rounds of disputes may be needed depending on the creditor's responses.

Can you guarantee results?

No ethical credit repair company can guarantee specific results because we don't control the credit bureaus or creditors. What we can guarantee:

  • We will dispute all inaccurate, unverifiable, or unfair items on your reports
  • We will use every legal tool available under the FCRA and FDCPA
  • We will be transparent about our progress and honest about your prospects
  • If we don't achieve any deletions in 90 days, you pay nothing (per-deletion pricing)

Beware of companies promising specific score increases or claiming they can remove accurate negative items ‚Äî these are red flags for scams.

What items can be removed from my credit report?

We can challenge and potentially remove:

  • Late payments that were actually on time
  • Charge-offs that are inaccurate or unverifiable
  • Collections that can't be validated
  • Bankruptcies with errors
  • Foreclosures with incorrect information
  • Judgments and liens that are inaccurate
  • Inquiries you didn't authorize
  • Duplicate entries
  • Accounts that don't belong to you
  • Items past the 7-10 year reporting limit

We cannot remove: Accurate, verifiable, and timely negative information. If you actually missed payments and the creditor can prove it, that information stays.

The Process

How does the credit repair process work?

  1. Free Consultation: We review your credit reports and discuss your goals.
  2. Analysis: We identify all potentially removable negative items.
  3. Dispute Preparation: We create custom dispute letters citing specific legal violations.
  4. Submission: Disputes are sent via certified mail to all relevant parties.
  5. Response Tracking: We monitor for responses within the 30-day window.
  6. Follow-up: If items aren't removed, we escalate with additional disputes or complaints.
  7. Results: You receive updated credit reports showing deletions.

Will I need to do anything during the process?

We handle the heavy lifting, but your participation is important:

  • Provide documents: Credit reports, identity verification, and any supporting documentation for disputes.
  • Review updates: Check your client portal for progress updates and respond to any requests.
  • Maintain good habits: Pay all current bills on time and avoid new negative marks while we work.
  • Communicate: Let us know about any creditor contacts or changes in your situation.

How do I get my credit reports?

You're entitled to free credit reports from all three bureaus:

  • AnnualCreditReport.com: Official free reports once per year
  • Direct from bureaus: During the dispute process, you'll receive updated reports automatically
  • Credit monitoring services: Many banks and credit cards now offer free scores and reports

We recommend pulling your reports before our consultation so we can review them together. During our service, you'll receive updated reports as part of the dispute process.

Can I do credit repair myself?

Yes, absolutely. Credit repair is a legal right, and you can dispute items yourself for free. Our clients choose us for several reasons:

  • Time: The process is time-consuming and requires follow-up
  • Expertise: We know the specific laws and language that gets results
  • Persistence: We handle multiple rounds of disputes and creditor communications
  • Strategy: We know which items to prioritize and how to sequence disputes

If you prefer the DIY route, we offer a consultation-only package with templates and guidance.

Your Credit Goals

How much will my score increase?

Score increases vary widely depending on your starting point and what gets removed. Some general guidelines:

  • Removing one major collection: 20-50 points
  • Removing late payments: 10-30 points per item
  • Multiple deletions: Can be 100+ points in some cases

Important: We focus on approval odds, not just scores. A mortgage lender cares about your full credit profile, not just a number.

Can you help me get approved for a mortgage?

Yes, this is one of our specialties. Different loan programs have different requirements:

  • FHA loans: Minimum 580 credit score, more flexible with collections and disputes
  • Conventional loans: Typically need 620+, stricter requirements
  • VA loans: No minimum score, but lenders often want 620+

We tailor our strategy to your target loan program and timeline. If you're planning to buy in 6-12 months, we can create a plan to get you mortgage-ready.

Should I pay off collections before applying for a loan?

It depends. Paying off a collection doesn't automatically remove it from your credit report ‚Äî the negative mark stays, just with a "paid" status.

For some loan programs (like FHA), you may need to pay off collections over a certain amount. For others, it doesn't matter.

Our approach:

  • First, try to remove the collection through dispute/validation
  • If it can't be removed, negotiate a "pay for delete" if possible
  • Only pay without deletion if your loan program requires it

We'll advise you on the best strategy for your specific situation.
