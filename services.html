Credit Disputes

Inaccurate information on your credit report can drag down your score and prevent you from qualifying for loans. We identify and dispute these errors with the credit bureaus and creditors.

What We Dispute:

  • Late payments that were actually on time
  • Accounts that don't belong to you
  • Duplicate entries of the same debt
  • Incorrect account balances or credit limits
  • Outdated negative items past the reporting period
  • Bankruptcies, judgments, or liens with errors

How It Works:

  1. Analysis: We review all three credit reports (Equifax, Experian, TransUnion) to identify potentially inaccurate items.
  2. Documentation: We gather evidence and prepare dispute letters citing specific laws (FCRA, FDCPA).
  3. Submission: Disputes are sent to credit bureaus and/or creditors via certified mail with tracking.
  4. Follow-up: We track responses and escalate if bureaus fail to investigate properly.
  5. Results: When items are removed or corrected, you pay only for successful deletions.

Debt Validation

Under the Fair Debt Collection Practices Act (FDCPA), you have the right to demand that debt collectors prove you actually owe the debt they're reporting. Many can't ‚Äî and must remove the item.

When to Use Debt Validation:

  • Unknown collection accounts on your credit report
  • Debts sold to multiple collection agencies
  • Amounts that don't match your records
  • Old debts that may be past the statute of limitations
  • Any collection account you're unsure about

The Validation Process:

We send formal debt validation letters within 30 days of first contact (or immediately for existing collections). Collectors must provide:

  • Proof they own the debt
  • The original contract or agreement
  • Complete payment history
  • Documentation showing the amount is accurate
Important: If a collector cannot validate the debt, they must cease collection efforts and remove the item from your credit report. This is a powerful tool for cleaning up collections.

Credit Building

Removing negative items is only half the battle. Adding positive credit history is essential for reaching your goals ‚Äî whether that's a 700+ score or qualifying for a mortgage.

Credit Building Strategies:

1. Secured Credit Cards

Start with cards that require a security deposit but report like regular credit cards. We recommend specific cards with no annual fees and upgrade paths.

2. Credit Builder Loans

Small installment loans designed specifically to build credit. Payments are reported to all three bureaus.

3. Authorized User Status

Strategic use of becoming an authorized user on someone else's established account to benefit from their positive history.

4. Rent Reporting

Services that report your rent payments to credit bureaus, adding positive payment history without new debt.

Important Considerations:

  • Only open accounts you can manage responsibly
  • Keep credit utilization under 30% (ideally under 10%)
  • Never miss a payment ‚Äî even one late payment hurts
  • Be patient ‚Äî building credit takes time, but it's worth it
